The Swivel Squatter

a chair for inflexible elementary school teachers

In my Design Engineering course, we were tasked to design a chair tailored for the needs of a specific persona.

  • Project Type: course team project
  • Time: 3 weeks
  • Role: researcher, project manager, designer

the persona

Vilde, age 37, elementary school teacher

Vilde is inflexible, so sitting on the floor during class gives her hip pain.

She wants a chair that allows her to sit comfortably, gives her maximum mobility, and positions her close to her students.

the question

How might we produce a classroom-friendly chair that accommodates Vilde’s lack of flexibility?

research - kinesiology

We looked for suggestions from professionals like physical therapists and yoga instructors, and we learned that…

For people with limited hip flexibility, sitting on the floor creates a narrow angle between the hip and the femur, causing instability and back pain

Joe Miller, "How to sit comfortably on the floor"

The most common way to reduce hip flexion demand is to elevate the hips.

ideation!

After individual brainstorming, each team member pitched their designs. Here are some of the designs that we discussed:

Includes a low rocking chair, a foldable chair that works like a bendy straw, and what is basically a child-proofed magis spun chair
Includes a spinning, rounded yoga block, and a chair that assists the user to stand up and sit down
Includes a foldable chair that is inspired by MOFT laptop stands

final design

    “T-shaped” base with padded wings allows the user to kneel comfortably Tilt mechanism at the joints allow adjustment to tilt angle of seat and armrest Swivel mechanism placed between base/seat, and base/arm-rest allowing the user to vary sitting position as desired Push-Button height adjustment for armrest Rounded edges to increase impact safety

prototyping

With these design choices in mind, we created a physical prototype

CAD analysis & structural support

Our first CAD prototype lacked structural support, so materials warped under stress of applied forces

We increased structural support by adding flare of support at connection to both seat and base

conclusions & lessons

Take a step back and consider the most fundamental features first. I was overwhelmed by the vast number of choices that we need to make in the design process – the curves, the material, the swivel mechanisms. Eventually, a teammate guided us to take a step back and consider the most fundamental features of our chair, which made the task much more manageable. The ideation process is so chaotic! We struggled during our ideating stage, since our ideas were all so widely different. Eventually, we organized our thoughts by recording the features that we appreciated from each design and piecing them together. After this experience, I began to truly appreciate the “design squiggle”
